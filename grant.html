Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible for a no co-signer student loan?
International and domestic students admitted to eligible schools may apply without a co-signer, based on future earning potential and academic standing.
What are the benefits of applying without a co-signer?
You don't need a credit history or a guarantor – the loan decision is based on your program and expected income.
What documents do I need?
Typically your passport, acceptance letter, academic transcripts, and proof of funds for living expenses.
What are the loan repayment terms?
Repayment usually starts 6 months after graduation, with flexible terms up to 20 years depending on the loan.
Can I repay my loan early?
Yes, you can make early repayments without any prepayment penalties.
What are the benefits of choosing this loan?
Competitive interest rates, no co-signer required, and support for students from various backgrounds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible for scholarships?
Eligibility varies by scholarship – it may be based on academic merit, financial need, athletic ability, or specific demographics.
What documents are required?
Typically transcripts, letters of recommendation, personal essays, and proof of enrollment or acceptance.
When are scholarship deadlines?
Deadlines vary; many are between November and March for the following academic year. Always check specific scholarship details.
Can I apply for multiple scholarships?
Yes, you can and should apply for as many scholarships as you qualify for.
How are scholarship funds disbursed?
Funds are usually sent directly to your educational institution to cover tuition and fees; any excess may be refunded to you.
Do scholarships need to be repaid?
No, scholarships are gift aid and do not need to be repaid.
*Scholarship availability and amounts vary by institution and program. Additional terms and conditions apply.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a grant?
A grant is a form of financial aid that does not need to be repaid, typically based on financial need.
Who is eligible for grants?
Eligibility varies; many grants are need-based, but some are for specific groups (e.g., teachers, researchers).
What documents are needed?
Commonly the FAFSA, financial statements, and sometimes a project proposal or personal statement.
How do I apply for grants?
Most require submitting the FAFSA and possibly additional applications through your school or grant provider.
Can grants be renewed?
Some grants are renewable each year if you continue to meet eligibility requirements.
Do grants affect other aid?
Grants may reduce the amount of other aid you can receive; always consult your financial aid office.
*Grant availability and amounts depend on federal, state, and institutional funding. Additional terms apply.
